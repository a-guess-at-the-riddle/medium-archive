Deus dormit                        God sleeps
Et liberi ignem faciunt            And his sons start a fire,
Numquam extinguunt                 Which they cannot extinguish,
Ne expergisci possit               and he will never be able to awaken.

Omnia dividit                      Every tragedy divides,
Tragedia coram                     Before our very eyes,
Amandum quae                       those things which ought to be loved.

Et nocte perpetua                  And through this endless night,
In desperatione                    In despair,
Auroram videre potest              He can see the dawn,
Mane tempus expergiscendi.         Which will awake him the next morning.

--“Somnus”, Final Fantasy XV

(lyrics: Tetsuya Nomura. Latin transl: Taro Yamashita & Kazuhiro Komiya)

In the divide between the classical & the quantum, the local & the nonlocal, between magic & science and tradition & change, there’s a Twilight Realm and a poised king — in between all things.

turned against himself
pushing himself through himself

in between himself
reconciling Self

three paths become a superpath
self-oscillation becomes a superpendulum
in between everything

alone free // free alone.

Can we reach such a poised state so as to win our freedom?

As with the meditative practice of body-scanning through a sequence of subharmonics in a movement-dance of focus, we self-oscillate between awareness & attention. The stable path of sequence bifurcations seems as if it might result in our skipping stone being brought to life (higher-self)? What will remain? — a small mercurial remnant hidden among its higher-order self? — higher-order formlessness (superformless)
